# Corruption and Fell Sorcery

Sorcery is volatile and warps its practitioners. Demonic corruption is everywhere in the Shattered Lands. 

Your corruption score can be increased by unleashing sorcerous powers, using cursed relics, or committing heinous acts. A newly created character has a zero corruption score.

As your Corruption score increases, you suffer increasingly unpleasant effects.

## Corruption Check 

You’ll make a check when directed by the GM or whenever you gain corruption. Roll d20. If the result is equal to or greater than your corruption score, you hold chaos at bay (this time) and suffer no consequences.

Otherwise, roll a subsequent d20, add your corruption score, and consult the Mark of Chaos table for the effect.

## Death from Corruption

After gaining a **fifth** Aspect of the same kind, you are warped into a monster, pose an immediate threat to your adventuring part, and a long-term threat to the game world. The GM takes over your character. It’s time to mint a new adventurer.

- **Snake**: your character fully transforms into a horrible serpentine malison, vowing enmity on all who trusted you.
- **Demon**: your character gains mastery over souls, seeking to ensorcell and enthrall the region’s leaders and beyond.
- **Stranger**: your character crystallizes into an obsidian pillar of hideous architecture, corrupting the landscape.
