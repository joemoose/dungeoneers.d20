# Character Advancement

## What Do You Do Next?

The goal of an Expedition is most often to learn about a mysterious environment, evade a powerful threat, or seek out mysterious artifacts.

A successful expedition is one that returns to Civilisation with something to show for it. This could be a lost relic, a terrible secret, or just a good story.

## Experience Levels

When a character has completed the requirements, they move to the next *Experience Level*.

Each time they advance a level, roll 3d6 for HP and each of their Attribute Scores. If the roll is higher than your current score, it is increased by 1 to a maximum of 18.

**Reputation**: As your character advances through Experience Levels, they may also be treated differently by people they encounter.

| Level | Requirement |
| - | - |
| Novice | You are ready to go on an expedition. |
| Professional | You have survived at least one expedition. |
| Expert | You have survived at least three dangerous expeditions since reaching the Professional Level. |
| Veteran | You have survived at least five expeditions since reaching Expert Level and have taken on an Apprentice.
| Master | You have an apprentice, at least at the expert level, and have survived a dangerous expedition with them since reaching the veteran level. |
| Beyond | Upon reaching Master Level, a character may have greater ambitions. This could be a desire for a legacy, engagement in an enterprise, rulership over a domain, or immortality. Your character might retire or become an NPC controlled by the GM, and your apprentice becomes your primary character. It's up to you. |
