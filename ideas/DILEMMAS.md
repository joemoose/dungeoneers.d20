DILEMMAS 

Whenever the characters get close to getting something they want, that leaves an exciting narrative hook and offers a tough choice in the form of two desirable or undesirable outcomes.

They may pick one or push for both, making a save, sacrificing something, or battling their wits.

This system gives the referee the narrative ability to give partial success and hard failures from story games.

---

**Into the Odd** doesn't have many moving parts, but your game should have lots of exciting decisions to be made. For exciting choices, you need to:

1. Give enough information.
2. Have multiple reasonable actions.
3. Give actions consequences.

A particular type of choice is a **Dilemma**, the tough choice.

When ruling a Dilemma, remember the mantra **Pick or Push.**

Present two desirable or equally undesirable choices.

The players either **Pick** one OR **Push** for both.

If they **Pick** one, then they get exactly that, but they miss out on the other choice. Don't over-complicate the choice with too many hidden consequences. Keep it simple between two things they want.

If they **Push** for both, they'll have to do one of the following:

- **Risk**: Try something risky, ranging from a single Saving Throw to a full-on adventure.
- **Sacrifice**: Give up something else in return for both, which is usually a resource. Throwing time or money at a problem is usually the easy way out, so make sure those have real consequences.
- **Smarts**: Have a clever idea that lets them get both without losing out. Generally, I'm easily talked into about this, especially if it's funny. There may still be minor consequences.

**An Important Note**

There aren't binary choices in RPGs. The players are still deciding on an action and might do something entirely different. This system is just a framework for getting them to make tough choices, so don't discourage them from going off-grid. Most often, these are just one of the types of Push presented in the bullets above.

**Examples**

For our example, we'll say the group is leading an expedition across the sea to find a mythical vortex to paradise. At various points, I could imagine these pick-or-push moments arising.

**Preparing for the Expedition**

You have only an hour left before you need to head to your ship, and you can't be in two places at once.

- **Extra Information:** Head to the library and finish reading that guide to ocean storms you were told to read.

**OR**

- **Extra Resources:** You never visited that wealthy relative who promised to help fund your expedition. He'll pay a share of the bill if you go and visit him.

**Possible Pushes**

- Risk: Steal the book from the Library before seeing your Uncle (possibly DEX Save).
- Sacrifice: Tell the expedition to hold on while you do both, losing travel time.
- Smarts: Take the book and extort the librarian for that extra money. (Really closer to risk, but too good to pass up.)

**Keeping Morale Up**

It's been a good few weeks at sea now. The crew are bickering; they're sick of the same old food, and they've caught onto the fact that you guys aren't exactly seasoned sailors. You could:

- **Make them Happy Now:** Let's crack out some extra rum rations and have a beach party on the next island. They'll love you for now, but they'll be upset later when the rum runs dry.

**OR**

- **Make them Happy Later:** Look, things are tough, but we have to press on if we're going to get to our destination. They'll be grumpy for now, but if you can reach your destination, they'll be glad we stayed the course.

**Possible Pushes**

- Risk: Try to make a rousing speech (possibly WIL Save).
- Sacrifice: Give them a rest day. They'll be happy, but you're wasting time.
- Smarts: A work-dance. These guys love dancing right, so work some swabbing and knot-tying into their moves.


**Other Pick or Push Moments on a Big Expedition**

**Getting a Ship:** It's a Good Ship, OR it won't Bankrupt you.

**Hiring a Crew:** They're Loyal, OR Total Bad-asses.

**Travel:** Be Fast OR Be Safe.
