# Dragonpowder Gonnes

Dragonpowder gonnes are expensive, exotic, and very loud. Wielded by the rich or the foolhardy, these strange weapons are reputed to be of Dwarven manufacture. However, they could very well hail from the infernal planes.

All dragonpowder gonnes have the following qualities:

- **Ammunition**: Remove one shot from your total ammo after reloading. Some gonnes require more than a single shot per reload.
- **Armor piercing**: Damage ignores up to 4 armor.
- **Unwieldy**: Unlike all other weapons, gonnes first require either a DEX *or* WIL Save to hit. If the Save succeeds, roll damage. If the Save fails, the attack misses. In either event, the gonne must be reloaded before firing again. Rainy or windy conditions impose Disadvantage on the Save.
	- On a **Critical Success**, the gonne ignores all the target’s armor.
	- On a **Critical Failure**, the gonne misfires. Roll on the Misfire Table to determine the results.
- **Deadly**: When rolling for damage, on a **Critical Hit**, rather than ignoring all armor as with other weapons, roll damage a second time. If another critical hit occurs, roll damage a third time, and so on.
- **Long-range**: If you can see the target, you can attack it. However, you cannot attack targets in the same zone as you. The exception is the Dragon Lance; see the following Gonnes Table.
- **Reloading**: Gonnes require an action and a successful DEX *or* WIL Save to reload. You can’t reload a gonne while moving or if an enemy is in the same zone. Mark off one ammo usage after successfully reloading.
	- On a **Critical Success**, you may attack in the same action as reloading.
	- On a **Critical Failure**, mark off one (wasted) ammo usage.
- **Noisy**: Igniting dragonpowder has loud and explosive results. Lesser creatures must make a Morale Check when hearing the shot. Anything more intelligent will notice and investigate.

Remember, if you can buy dragonpowder gonnes, so can everyone else.

## Gonne Misfires

On a Critical Failure to fire a dragonpowder gonne, roll 2d6 and refer to the following Misfire table.

| 2d6 | Result |
| :-: | :----- |
| 2 | The gonne explodes and is destroyed, dealing damage to you. |
| 3 | You catch on fire, taking d6 damage per round, ignoring armor until you put it out. |
| 4–5 | You get sparks in your eyes. All dice rolls have Disadvantage until you take an action and make a STR Save. |
| 6–8 | The gonne fails to fire. You must take one round of fiddling with the mechanism and make a WIL Save if you want to try firing it again. |
| 9–10 | The gonne breaks. It will need to be thoroughly cleaned and repaired before being used again. The repair cost is 30% of its price. |
| 11 | The gonne is fine, but everyone close by must make a LUCK Save. The shot hits the character who fails and rolls the highest. |
| 12 | The gonne explodes and is destroyed, dealing *blast* damage to you and everyone nearby. |
[Misfire Table]
