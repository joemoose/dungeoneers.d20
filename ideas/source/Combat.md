# Combat

Combat is dangerous, and wise adventurers seek to avoid it. Still, sometimes violence is inevitable. 

## Initiative

Before combat commences, the GM determines if a side is surprised. If so, that side does not act in the first round of the battle.

Afterward, players make an **Initiative check** at the start of each round. To make an Initiative check, each player chooses whether to attack with a weapon or take another action.

If they choose to attack with a weapon, they declare which weapon they use and roll a **d20**. If the result is **equal to** or **less than** their weapon’s **Speed**, that character acts **before the enemy**. If the result is **higher**, they act **after** the enemy has acted.

Otherwise, use a **default Speed** of **10**.

## Actions

During a round, a character can move from one zone to an adjacent zone and perform a single action. This action can be anything within reason, such as attacking, negotiating, making another move to an adjacent zone, or casting a spell.

## Attacking

**Attacks always hit**. Declare a target and roll your weapon’s damage dice. The result is damage to your target’s HP minus its Armor Rating (AR). Remember, this is true for your opponents as well. Their attacks will always hit you.

### Critical Hits

Rolling **maximum damage** on a weapon’s damage dice is a critical hit. It ignores the target’s AR and deals the total damage.

### Dual-Wielding

If dual-wielding a set of weapons, use the **lowest** Speed among them to determine initiative, but roll damage for both and choose which single result to keep.

### Multiple Attackers 

If more than one character attacks the same target, attackers must roll their Damage together and keep only the highest single die. An extra point of damage is added to the result for each additional attacker.

### Ranged Attacks

Ranged weapons cannot be used if you are in the same zone as the target.

### Aiming

Using an action to aim instead of attacking allows a character to ignore the target’s AR with a ranged weapon the next Round.

### Blast

Attacks with the *blast* quality affect all targets in the zone, rolling separately for each affected combatant. Blast refers to anything from explosions, to massive cleaving onslaughts, to the impact of a meteorite. If unsure how many targets can be affected in the zone, roll the related damage die for a result.

### Bonus Damage

Attacks may receive a bonus, noted as +d6, +d8, and so on. Roll the extra die with your damage roll, keeping the best result. Attacks against helpless or unaware targets always receive +d12 Bonus Damage.

### Impaired

Impaired attacks, such as firing through cover or fighting unarmed, roll d4 Damage only and cannot receive Bonus Damage.

### Mounted

Unmounted attacks against mounted targets in the same zone are at a disadvantage.

### Disengaging

If you want to move to another zone while an enemy occupies your zone, you must make a successful BRAWN check. If you fail, the enemy makes a free attack before you move out of the zone.

A check is not required if you have allies in the zone that have not yet taken an action.

### Stunts

For other possible actions involving an opposing combatant, like pushing a combatant into another zone, disarming, or grappling them, the party most at risk should make a relevant check to see if the action succeeds.

### Backpack Items

Retrieving an item from a backpack slot during combat requires an action.

## Damage

When a combatant takes Damage, they lose that much HP. If they take more Damage than their remaining HP, they are injured, and any remaining Damage is removed from their BRAWN Score. They must pass a BRAWN Save using the new Score to avoid Critical Damage.

### Armor

==REWRITE==

Armor subtracts its rating from the result of any Damage rolls against the wearer. The maximum armor rating is 6.

### Critical Damage

If a character suffers Critical Damage, they cannot do anything but crawl slowly until they are tended to by an ally and have a Short Rest. They’ll die of their injuries if left unattended for an hour.

## Scars

When a character’s HP is reduced to zero or less, they are uniquely scarred. Look up the result in the table below based on the total amount of damage:

| Damage | Scar |
| :-: | - |
| 1 | **Lasting Scar**: Roll 1d6; 1: Neck, 2: Hands, 3: Eye, 4: Chest, 5: Legs, 6: Ear. Roll 2d4. If the total is higher than your max HP, swap it. |
| 2 | **Rattling Blow**: You’re disoriented and shaken. BRAINS checks have a disadvantage until you heal and rest. Roll 2d4. If the total is higher than your max HP, swap it. |
| 3 | **Walloped**: You’re sent flying, landing flat on your face, winded. Add a Fatigue to your inventory. You are *deprived* until you rest. Add 1 to your max HP. |
| 4 | **Broken Limb**: Roll 1d6; 1-2: Leg, 3-4: Arm, 5: Rib, 6: Skull. BRAWN checks have a disadvantage until you heal and rest. Once mended, roll 4d4. If the total is higher than your max HP, swap it. |
| 5 | **Diseased**: You’re afflicted with a revolting, uncomfortable infection. BRAINS and BRAWN checks have a disadvantage until you heal and rest. When you get over the infection, roll 4d4. If the total is higher than your max HP, swap it. |
| 6 | **Reorienting Head Wound**: Roll 1d6; 1–3: BRAINS, 4–6: BRAWN. Roll 3d6. If the total is higher than your current ability score, swap it. |
| 7 | **Hamstrung**: You can’t walk until you heal and rest. After recovery, roll 3d6. If the result is higher than your BRAWN Score, swap it. |
| 8 | **Deafened**: You cannot hear anything until you heal and rest. Afterward, Roll 3d6. If the result is higher than your BRAINS Score, swap it. |
| 9 | **Re-brained**: Some remote part of your psyche is knocked loose. Roll 3d6. If the result is higher than your BRAINS Score, swap it. |
| 10 | **Sundered**: An appendage is torn off, crippled, or useless. The GM will tell you which. Roll 3d6. If the result is higher than your BRAWN Score, swap it. |
| 11 | **Mortal Wound**: You are *deprived* and out of the action. Add a Fatigue to your inventory. You will die shortly unless stabilized or healed. Upon recovery, roll 4d4. Take the new result as your max HP. |
| 12+ | **Doomed**: Death seemed imminent, but somehow, luckily, you survived. If your next Save against Critical Damage fails, you die immediately and horrifically (and rather messily). If you pass, roll 3d6. If the result is higher than your LUCK Score, swap it. |

## Attribute Loss and Death

==REWRITE==

If a character has their BRAWN reduced to zero, they are dead. If BRAINS are reduced to zero, they are mad and take unreasonable actions. If LUCK is reduced to zero, they suffer constant, bizarre mishaps and cannot accomplish anything significant.

If your PC dies, make a new one, and the GM will introduce them as quickly as possible.

## Deprivation and Fatigue

==REWRITE==

If Deprived of a crucial need, your character can’t recover HP or Attributes. For each day Deprived, a character adds Fatigue to their Inventory, which occupies one Slot and lasts until they take a Long Rest. Spells and other events may also cause Fatigue.

## Rest and Healing

==REWRITE==


There are three ways your character can rest and recover.

- **Short Rest**: Takes 1 Turn. A swig of water and a few minutes of rest will restore all HP.
- **Long Rest**: Takes 1 Watch. A meal and some sleep will restore all HP. This will also restore d6 to BRAINS or BRAWN and remove all Fatigue.
- **Full Rest**: Takes a week back in safety. Fully restores BRAINS and BRAWN and removes most long-term conditions.
