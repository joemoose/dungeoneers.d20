# Archetypes

There are three basic archetypes: warrior, thief, and sorcerer. These archetypes add unique features to your character, but they are only a starting point.

A single class does not limit your character’s role or skills. Instead, the equipment they carry and their experiences define their specialty. Attributes, skills, and related saves do not determine your character. They are just tools.

Look not to your character sheet for answers. Instead, be creative with your intuition, items, and connections. Asking questions and listening to details is more valuable than any stat.

## Warrior

Fighters are adventurers dedicated to mastering the arts of combat and war. In a group of adventurers, the role of fighters is to battle monsters and defend other characters.

### Combat Maneuvers

#### Level 1 Talent

You know a variety of special maneuvers you can perform in combat. You do this by spending a resource called grit. You start with one grit. At every even level, you gain one additional grit. You may use multiple maneuvers at the same time.

The grit you spend is restored after you take a long rest.

- **Focus**: When making an attack, you may spend one grit to ignore the target’s Armor.
- **Not on my watch!**: You may spend one grit to re-roll your Speed roll when determining combat order.
- **Shake it off**: Whenever you take damage, you may spend one grit 
- to reduce the damage by 1d6.

#### Level 2, 5, & 7 Maneuvers

At the 2nd, 5th, and 7th levels, you may choose one of the maneuvers below and add them to your repertoire.

- **Command**: Instead of making an attack, you may spend one grit to allow an ally to make an attack that deals +1d6 additional damage.
- **Payback**: When an ally takes damage from an enemy within the range of your weapon, you may spend one grit to attack them immediately.
- **Shield**: When an ally you share a zone with takes damage, you may spend a grit and add your AR to theirs for this Round (this can increase it above 6).
- **Split fire**: When you use a ranged weapon, you may spend one grit to shoot at two separate targets at once.
- **Taunt**: You may spend one grit to make all enemies attack you this Round.
- **Rally**: At the start of a Round, spend one grit to give you and your allies +1 additional damage until the end of the current Round.
- **Cleave**: Instead of making a normal attack, you may spend one grit to make a single attack hitting up to four enemies in your zone. This attack deals half damage but still ignores Armor on a critical hit.

#### Level 3 Feature

**Trophies**

After you defeat a creature in combat, you may take a trophy from their remains. A trophy takes up one item slot.

When you encounter a creature of which you carry a trophy, you may spend one grit to:

- Receive +d6 bonus damage when attacking it.
- Gain an advantage on a check against that creature’s abilities.

**Spoor**

You can also construct a trophy by scouting a creature’s lair and taking something from it or spending at least a week researching the creature using adequate resources. This spoor trophy is destroyed after it is used once.

## Thief

Thieves are adventurers who live by their skills of deception and stealth. Their range of unique skills makes them very handy companions in adventures.

### Talents

You have a variety of unique talents. When you create your character, and each time you gain a level, you may choose a talent from below or gain mastery of a skill you already have.

**BACKSTAB**: When you attack a creature that isn’t aware of you, in addition to the advantage you would typically gain, you double the damage rolled.

- **Mastery**: During combat, as part of your attack, you may make a BRAWN check against a creature aware of you to confuse or flank the target, adding a +d12 damage bonus.

**EXPERTISE**: Once per session, you may re-roll an ability check.

- **Mastery**: You may re-roll any die instead.

**MANY POCKETS**: It no longer takes an action to retrieve an item from your backpack slots during combat.

- **Mastery**: You gain two additional backpack slots.

**NETWORK**: Make a BRAINS check when entering a new settlement. If you succeed, note that you have a contact in the settlement. The nature of this contact is up to you and the GM and can be fleshed out whenever the need arises.

- **Mastery**: You may make a BRAINS check once each session to see if you have a contact in an area, regardless of whether you’re new to the area.

**SUPPLIES**: In a settlement, you may acquire one unit of Supplies. A Supply is an abstract item with a single use available only to the thief that takes up an inventory slot. It can be changed into any commonly available mundane item whenever you want.

- **Mastery**: Supplies can be used three times before needing to be re-acquired in a settlement.

**SWIFT**: You no longer trigger a free attack when disengaging.

- **Mastery**: You always act before the enemy if you’re not surprised and not wearing heavy armor or wielding a shield.

## Sorcerer

## Talents

**Sorcereous Powers**: You alter the world through sheer force of will and your body’s consumption. You need no charms, runes, spellbooks, or scrolls. Reality is yours to command.

Your sorcerous power requires you to expend (bleed) at least one point of BRAWN or BRAINS as essence to fuel your magic. The more of your body you bleed, the greater the essence consumed and the greater the magnitude of the power unleashed.

Ability scores lost in invoking your powers are healed as usual—long rests, medical attention, etc. A critical damage check is not required when bleeding out the essence. An invocation derives all its essence from bleeding only one ability score—you cannot spread your sacrifice across BRAWN and BRAINS.

**To invoke a sorcerous power**, bleed essence and make a BRAINS check. If the check succeeds, the power manifests as you intended. The power fails to materialize on a failure, and you risk corruption.

The check has an advantage if the invocation is performed over a turn rather than a combat round. The GM may allow the invocation to succeed automatically when invoking your power uninterrupted as a ritual during a watch. 

### Powers

When you create your character, pick one power. You may select an additional power at the 3rd, 5th, and 7th levels.

#### Alter

Make a declarative statement affecting one creature or object you can see. The statement is true for [essence] combat rounds. The creation cannot cause damage directly (use Harm), move a creature or object, or create new things or effects (use Create). Creatures and magical objects can save to avoid being altered.

The maximum size or mass of the creature or object to be altered is that of the sorcerer. You can double the size or mass altered for each additional essence bled. 

> "This door does not exist." You can walk through the space the door formerly occupied.  
> "The giant is now a mole.” The giant retains its abilities and HP, but it might be easier to surround it and attack.  
> "The dragon is made of paper." The dragon is now made of paper. It retains its abilities and HP, but it will take extra damage if you set it on fire with a torch.  

#### Create

Bring forth a creation. The creature or object created exists for [essence] rounds. The creation is, at most, the size or mass of the sorcerer. For each additional essence bled, you can double the size or mass of your creation. 

More essence can be bled to imbue the creation with specific properties or powers beyond its basic form. Creations cannot deal damage directly (use Harm), nor are they sentient. You can create objects with magical effects (flying carpets, invisibility cloaks), but these objects do not deal magic damage or provide permanent results. For example, HP restored from an invoked healing potion lasts only for the duration of your invocation.

> Zathar the Sorcerer invokes a flying horse, which he mounts to escape. He bled six essences (the creation is *2 times his size* and can *fly* for *3 rounds*).

#### Harm

Deal an [essence] number of d6 damage to one target creature or object you can see. Creatures and magical objects can make a save to avoid or mitigate the damage. Describe your manifestations however you’d like: electrical arcs, exudations of fire from your fingertips, grasping hands from the underworld. Bleed an essence for each additional target affected.

> Zathar bleeds two essences. He can Harm one creature for 2d6 damage or two creatures for 1d6 damage each.

#### Soul Vision

You can see the souls of an [essence] number of creatures for [essence] rounds. This allows you to guess the approximate location of invisible creatures. You can also immediately tell if a person is possessed, undead, protected by the gods, or a spellcaster. Bleed more essence to see additional souls or observe for more rounds.

#### Ward

Manifest a simmering, translucent shield around you or a target creature or object for [essence] turns. The ward provides an advantage on checks to avoid magical effects and impairs damage from physical attacks. The ward can be voluntarily sundered and destroyed to negate all damage from a single spell or attack.
