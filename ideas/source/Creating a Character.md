# Creating a Character

You are a newly minted adventurer: a tomb-raiding, treasure-seeking ne’er-do-well who wields a spellbook just as easily as a blade. You will live a dangerous life. You will delve into strange and mysterious places. With some luck, you might retire before dying.

## 1. Roll Attribute Scores

You have three Attribute Scores that describe your character’s basic strengths and weaknesses. Scores range from a minimum of 0 to a maximum of 18.

- **BRAINS**: Strength of will, awareness, and intellect.
- **BRAWN**: Physical strength, resilience, speed, and reflexes.
- **LUCK**: Good fortune, favor of the gods.

➡️  Roll **2d6+4** three times, noting the results. Assign the lowest value to your LUCK and the other two as you prefer between BRAINS and BRAWN.

## 2. Roll Hit Points

Your Hit Points (HP) are the total damage you can shrug off before suffering an injury. HP is quickly restored; an injury is more serious.

➡️  Roll 2d4 to determine your starting hit points.

## 3: Choose a Background

Now let’s find out a little backstory on your character.

➡️  Choose or randomly select a background from the following list. 

### Backgrounds

Your background describes your life before becoming an adventurer and grants skills and languages. 

#### Cultist

You are privy to obscure mysteries not known to outsiders. Did you break with your brothers and sisters, or do you still travel to further your cult’s tenets?

- **Skills**: Religion, Spirits, Rumors
- **Languages**: Trade Tongue, Vinčan

#### Houseborn

Born into a minor Merchant House, you’ve been trained to play markets and people since birth. Are you the last of a dying House or one of many kin with no other way to leave your mark on your House’s legacy?

- **Skills**: Influence, Politics, Trade
- **Languages**: Trade Tongue, Treden script

#### Muscle

Whether you were a gladiator, a hired thug, or a prestigious House’s guard, you know how to hold your own in a skirmish. Did you get tired of fighting for a master, or are you just looking for a new challenge?

- **Skills**: Intimidation, Brute Force, Tactics
- **Languages**: Trade Tongue, Argot

#### Nomad

You traveled the wilds with your tribe, away from the meddling of the Houses. Did you set out on a task given to you by your tribe’s elders, or were you banished from their tents and left to wander on your own?

- **Skills**: Healing, Survival, Herbalism
- **Languages**: Trade Tongue, an additional language of your choice.

#### Performer

You were part of a traveling troupe of actors, artists, and acrobats, showing off your skills to cheering crowds. Are you searching for inspiration for your next act or an audience more appreciative of your talents?

- **Skills**: Art, Performance, Acrobatics
- **Languages**: Trade Tongue

#### Scavenger

You are familiar with the dangers lurking in the shattered lands and how to get your hands on the hidden relics. Are you in it for the gold or to sate your curiosity?

- **Skills**: Exploring, Stealth, Traps
- **Languages**: Trade Tongue, Argot

#### Scribe

You spent years of your life inscribing and illuminating other people’s tales in dry tomes. Did you leave the scriptorium of your own accord, or were you forced to quit?

- **Skills**: History, Culture, Geography
- **Languages**: Trade Tongue, Treden script

#### Worker

A life of toiling in iron mines, working the fields, or building the strongholds and halls of House nobles has hardened your body and spirit. Did you take pride in your work, or are you happy to have left it behind?

- **Skills**: Endurance, Animals, Crafting 
- **Languages**: Trade Tongue

## 4. Choose an Archetype

Pick an archetype you want to play from the following section. The archetype grants your character special features, which you should note on your character sheet. For example, the warrior gains combat maneuvers.

## 5. Choose a Name

Give your character a name. Put some thought into how they look, their pronouns, their relationship with the other characters in the group, and how their background has influenced them to become the person they are at the start of the game. You don’t have to decide all this now; letting your character develop as you play is okay. Be careful not to get too attached; it’s a dangerous world.

Good luck, adventurer!
