## Alchemist

### Why Did You Stop Practicing?

1. **Lack of Talent**: Salt water was the most advanced concoction you could develop.
2. **Heresy**: Local clergy found your theories and practices blasphemous. They confiscated your works, and now you owe a minor indulgence to the church.
3. **Exiled**: You accidentally brewed a love potion instead of the requested routine salve. The subject was an heir to a local position of nobility, and their parent would rather see you dead than their offspring end up with you.
4. **Hunted**: Somehow, you created a homunculus, a miniature 6-inch replica of yourself. This creature hates you with every fiber of its being.
5. **Betrayed**: You’re pretty sure your mentor was slowly killing you with arsenic. You’ll find yourself in a vomiting fit once in a while.
6. **Accident**: The last concoction you brewed resulted in a massive explosion, scarring you horribly and burning down a significant portion of your home village, many of whom will never forgive you.

### What Were You Able To Make Away With?

1. **Alchemist’s Fire**: A clay bottle filled with sticky green fluid that catches fire when exposed to air. Counts a bottle of burning oil without needing to be lit.
2. **Bomb**: Head-sized iron ball filled with explosive powder with an attached wick. Deals d12 damage to everyone nearby it and can cause significant damage to structures it is placed nearby.
3. **Vial of Acid**: Can disintegrate 1 cubic inch of any metal. If used as a splash weapon, the target must pass a Save or suffer -1 to WIL if applied to the face, -1 DEX if applied to a limb, or -1 STR if applied to the torso.
4. **Tincture Of Truth-Talking**: Only a few drops remain, enough for one dose. This tasteless, transparent liquid will compel whoever ingests it to answer questions truthfully for d6 turns.
5. **Magnesium Shavings**: A pouch containing d6 uses that will easily ignite and burn intensely.
6. **Basic Alchemist’s Kit**: A wooden chest filled with four corked glass vials and stand, a small bronze weight scale, pestle & mortar, stirring rods, a bottle of solvent, and a pouch of salt.

## Begger

### What Led You To Your Lot?

1. **Family Business**: You were born into it. Your parents were raised as beggars, and you followed in their footsteps. At least one of your parents is still out there with their can and beggar’s bowl.
2. **Cheated**: Everything rode on that bet, and you were sure to win, but somehow the house was stacked against you. You swear you’ll get back at your rival someday. 
3. **Asceticism**: You follow a faith or philosophy that disallows you from owning more than a few things, needing to rely on the kindness of others. The organization you follow can be called on from time to time. 
4. **Profit**: It’s very lucrative; take the maximum amount of coins for a starting character.
5. **Doomed**: Ill omens followed your every step. You’ve never been able to hold steady work, but you’ve picked up on secrets others wish not to know.
6. **Beggar’s Guild**: You hold membership in a secret organization of spies and information brokers. You can tap into a network of unnoticed people who readily trade in knowledge.

### What Is The One Special Thing You Own?

1. **A Good Friend**: A faithful dog with a scent for extra scraps or an opportunity.
2. **Everdripping Cap**: One a day, when you turn your hat upside down, it will produce one pint of low-quality ale. Unfortunately, your hat isn’t resilient, so it’s always soggy and reeks of alcohol.
3. **Dire Catfish Catcher**: An impressively long fishing pole you swear can never snap, with an impressive tale about a fish just as big and resilient.
4. **Emergency Tooth**: A wooden tooth you can pop open. Useful for storing a few drops of a potion. Contains one store of a reasonably potent healing brew; restore d4 HP when you’re able to pop it open.
5. **Leomund’s Placid Pile**: A pile of rags you can crawl underneath, after which you find yourself in a pocket dimension—a small, modestly stocked hut within. Unfortunately, anyone who disturbs the pile ejects you from your cozy home.
6. **Spirit-Scaring Spit**: One small box of chewing tobacco that otherworldly beings find incredibly distasteful. For some minor spirits spewing the chew directly onto their face is a semi-reliable form of banishment. Only one use remains.

## Burglar

### Professional burglary is no longer part of your life. Why?

1. **Judged**: The last time you were caught, they took an ear; next time, you’ll probably be executed.
2. **Reformed**: The Acolytes of Law convinced you that your actions brought unholy Chaos into the world. Now you seek to pay for your past transgressions.
3. **Cursed**: Rumors that the last house you breached was a witch’s hut were entirely accurate. Now when you sleep, you have dreams of that house being hoisted on dozens of legs, crawling ever towards you.
4. **Soured**: While out robbing another, you returned to find your home had been burgled. Having tasted your own medicine, you found it very bitter and resolved to leave it behind.
5. **Pursued**: A bounty has been put on your head. You hope to find lands where no one recognizes you or you earn enough gold to repay your crimes.
6. **Bungled**: You were never good at it. You were continually caught in the act, assaulted by your targets, and repeatedly found yourself having to escape by leaping into manure piles. Maybe adventuring will be different?

### What was the most impressive thing you stole?

1. **Lucky Coin**: This coin always lands showing the side you choose.
2. **Monkey’s Paw**: An unattached paw of a monkey. When something is placed in its palm, the fingers wrap around it with an unbreakable grip. Squeezing the wrist stump causes the hand to release its grip.
3. **Fossilized Legend**: A palm-sized hunk of amber containing the miniature skeleton of a winged serpent.
4. **Makani’s Magnificent Messaging Cube**: An elaborately-decorate cube with a removable lid. Retains one sentence spoken into it and will replay the exact wording in the speaker’s voice once when opened.
5. **Coin-Purse of Copper Conversion**: A peculiar leather purse that will immediately convert any coin or gems placed within to copper, bearing the markings of the region where the owner is currently.
6. **Suspicious Song Bird**: A cage containing one warbler that will begin chirping when in the presence of a statement that is unlikely to be true.

## Butcher

### Why Did You Leave Butchery Behind?

1. **Bankruptcy**: Your practice failed to turn any profit.
2. **Guild Standards**: Things that the guild believed to be “unsanitary” and “unethical” were a significant part of your practice. You were stripped of your title.
3. **Hemophobia**: Despite being a fan of the art of the cut, the sight of blood leaves you faint.
4. **Cursed**: A nearby necromancer made it impossible for you to safely do your job on account of all the animals rising to eat your brains mid-butchering.
5. **Plant-Based Druidism**: Your community was run by a circle of Druids that made it very clear that if it wasn’t made of legumes, it wasn’t welcome.
6. **One Too Many Close Calls**: Having gained a wide array of scars, a lost finger, and the spilling of quite a bit of your blood, you thought plundering tombs sounded safer.

### What Strange Thing Did You Find On The Job?

1. **Cleaver of Returning**: Your knife is either enchanted or peculiarly crafted—it boomerangs back to you when thrown. Unfortunately, catching it is an extremely harrowing process.
2. **Bottle of Sumptuous Blood**: One of your prime cuts bled a liquid that makes any beast foam at the mouth. Pouring this blood out in the vicinity of animals will render them ravenous.
3. **A Highly Obedient Hog**: The one you couldn’t kill. You have a reliable pig following your every step.
4. **A Lucky Femur**: One of the bones of a cow you slaughtered was riddled with runes. Surely it provides you with some protection.
5. **Regenerative Grounds**: A fist full of ground beef grows a cubic inch of new beef daily. Does not preserve yesterday’s meat any longer, though.
6. **Nesting Best**: The last cow you butchered ended up being hollow, containing a sheep within. It walks and bleats like any other sheep and follows you around dutifully. You suspect that there’s an even smaller animal within.

## Charlatan

### What Drove You Out?

1. **Snakes**: The source of the oil you were intending to sell united and rose against you.
2. **Betrayal**: Your Mark sold you out to the Mercantile Guilds. Your old partner now lives comfortably, while you’ll never be able to sell anything again professionally.
3. **Vengeance**: The last town you swindled summoned a Law Spirit to get even with you.
4. **Guilt**: You honestly thought mercury lollipops would have strengthened the children.
5. **Deficiency**: You’ve gone through almost every idea in the book. Now, you’re adventuring for a sense of inspiration.
6. **Runner-Up**: An old rival always beats you to the punch—better pitch, more elaborate show, and they even made some money off it. You decided you were tired of being second-rate.

### What Did You Keep From Your Past?

1. **Pizazz**: A small crate of fireworks is left from your smoke and mirrors performances.
2. **A Very Vicious Pug**: You often dressed it up in fake leathery wings and imitation horns, then you’d “exorcise” the little imp with ancient chants and hidden sausages.
3. **Rock oil**: One pint of grease that only lubricates minerals.
4. **Cassel’s Clever Cure**: A potion that will cure any disease but unfortunately causes a different, completely random one.
5. **Soap**: A by-product of your usual platform, you have a nearly endless supply of lye.
6. **Invisibility Balm**: One tin of a fantastic lotion that turns a person invisible. The user’s skin becomes completely transparent, but the skeleton and organs remain opaque.

## Cleric

### Why did you depart from the church?

1. **Misinterpretations**: A villager conducted a seance with their recently deceased. It turns out religious counseling by you led to their direct damnation.
2. **Illiteracy**: You were able to trick your superiors into thinking you could read by staring absently into the illuminated texts for hours, but when asked to discuss readings from the scriptures, you were only able to give the most ridiculous of guesses.
3. **Dark Night**: Confronted by the stipulations of faith, you found yourself dizzied with doubts. Maybe you can find enlightenment in the relics hidden by past civilizations.
4. **Imposter**: It turns out that the particular deity worshipped by your church was just some diminutive fae creature playing a joke on humankind.
5. **Greed**: A confessor admitted to breaking into a local crypt and swooned at the glittering treasures buried within but fled when something skulked in the shadows. You know where this crypt is located.
6. **Corruption**: You discovered several plots within the church—ploys to steal from peasants, absolutions provided to nobles in exchange for wealth, and even ritualistic pleas to dark powers. Disaffected, you turned to a life on the road.

### What final gift were you given?

1. **Bracelet of the Truthful Tongue**: A wrist-sized loop of wooden beads. When worn in the presence of a lie, the twine will snap, spilling the beads.
2. **Silver Aspergillum**: A finely fashioned wand that allows the wielder to sprinkle holy water over nearby subjects.
3. **Clewd’s Catalog of Chaotic Creatures**: A leather-bound tome containing dozens of descriptions of demonic beings.
4. **Dentures of the Saint**: A small pouch of wooden teeth rumored to have been the eating implements of a notable saint. Whether this is true, they at least look holy to you.
5. **Incense of the Ascended Man**: A stick of sweet-smelling incense supposedly used by the famous ascetic during meditation that led to their apotheosis. Objects around 10 coins in weight will float on the fragrant smoke of the incense hundreds of feet up, never to be seen again.
6. **Vampire Hunting Kit, Deluxe Edition**: A lavishly polished wooden box containing cloves of garlic, vials of holy water, a small mirror, 3 wooden stakes, a mallet, and a scroll containing one Revocation of Invitation scroll bearing a bishop’s seal.

## Cook

### What killed your love for cooking?

1. **Curse**: The curse laid upon the land by the Rasping Beast of the Wood makes it pretty hard to cook anything when all the egg yolks have been transformed into curdled blood and the fish scream with every bite.
2. **Etiquette**: You had no idea that the herbs you picked for the Druid staying as your lord’s guest were parts of the dryad he loved. You snuck away in the night, fearing his retribution.
3. **Repulsion**: The delicacies of the nobles have taken a turn for the unsettling. No longer will you produce pickled gnome eyes or goblin marrow pudding!
4. **Framed**: Your lord was stricken blind after eating one of your Grete Pyes, but you swear it was that rascal of a squire who was always sneaking into your kitchen that poisoned them.
5. **Boredom**: A severe lack of interest in the local cuisine exhausted you. Maybe plundering the kitchen of an abandoned wizard’s tower will unveil new realms of flavor.
6. **Mishap**: You don’t know if perhaps the herbs you picked grew too close to the fairy stones or your interpretation of the recipe accidentally resulted in a spell, but the soup you prepared became animated. It curses your name to this day for giving it its horrendous existence.

### What keepsake reminds you of the old days?

1. **Fairly Exquisite Spice Kit**: Tins, jars, and pouches full of cardamon, ginger, nutmeg, sugar, and other spices. Maybe even a pinch of saffron.
2. **The Lord’s Grain**: Before leaving your lord’s employment, you snatched a wheelbarrow, took it into the stores, and filled it with flour before sprinting out into the wilds.
3. **Bardic creature**: A catfish you were about to gut looked at you directly and began to sing the most beautiful song you have ever heard. You now carry it in a large iron pot full of water.
4. **Prophetic Fruit**: A hedge wizard traded for one of your pies and gave you a supposedly magical apple. He claimed that if you ask the apple a question and take a bite from this apple, the answer will appear in the browning of the flesh.
5. **Peach Pie of the Sempiternal Scent**: A delicious pie that always smells as if it was just pulled from the oven, always a temperature that is just shy of hot.
6. **A Miraculous Recipe**: Stolen or accidentally stumbled upon, you have the idea for a miraculous flat circle of bread covered in pasted tomato, cheeses, and sliced spiced sausage. It could be famous someday if you get motivated and find the correct ratios.

## Cultist

### Why did you leave the flock?

1. **Purity**: Despite wanting to be in the hip crowd of fabulous chaos cultists and able to hang with all the bad boys and girls, it turns out your soul is incorruptible. When your cult finally accumulated enough virginal blood to summon the Festering Plague Goat, it shrieked when it gazed upon you, disgusting even the Fetid Lord.
2. **Ignorance**: You didn’t realize everyone was serious about this whole “bring on the apocalypse” thing; you thought it was just a social club to wear spooky robes and practice cackling.
3. **Escape**: Unable to produce more food for your vampire patron, they turned to feed on the cult itself. As you fled, you heard screams and wings fluttering behind you.
4. **Disappointment**: After your Dark Messiah manifested, they just hung around and were always eating your food, asking to crash in your hut. They were always promising dark gifts, but they needed to get back on their feet if you could spare a few silver pieces.
5. **Bargain**: Arrested for your dastardly deeds, the sheriff offered you a choice between the gallows or ratting out your snake-worshipping buddies. You chose freedom, although you’re sure the old arch-priest knows it was you who snitched.
6. **Revelation**: The whole cult scene goes much deeper than you ever expected. Many more heinous entities dwell in the dark, and you want to work your way up (or down?) the infernal ladder.

### What do you still have from the cult?

1. **Infernal Parasite**: One of your deity’s demonic messengers stabbed your stomach with its scorpion tail. You now have a demonic bot-fly living in your abdomen that will sometimes add its shriek to any war cry you can do, creating a chilling dissonance.
2. **Pristine Milk**: Your cult’s mascot turned out to be a top-notch dairy animal. You still have a tin of some of the finest goat milk.
3. **Horrifying Mask**: The bronze face is of some contorted, twisting maw of teeth, tongues, and eyes. Wearing it generally creeps others, and sometimes yourself, out.
4. **Cacophonous Flute**: A wreck of a flute; if you can play it for a considerable amount of time, everyone involved must save vs. migraine. Assuming they get beyond the initial annoyance and allow you to continue playing.
5. **Glyphed Parchment**: You took a scroll from the sacrifice room of your cult. You never learned to decipher its runes, but perhaps you could find someone who can.
6. **Unholy Priest’s Signet Ring**: Thinking you could pawn this quickly, everyone you have shown the ring so far gasps and ushers you away from their stall immediately.

## Gambler

### Why made you decide to stop being dealt in?

1. **Blessing**: You sold your soul to a Fortune Demon to never lose a bet, and it’s, unfortunately, looking for you to return your end of the deal.
2. **Luck**: You don’t know if you were cursed or just born without luck, but you routinely lose every big bet you make. You did gain a crew in your hometown who always invited you to their games, though.
3. **Sorrow**: After Ol’ Major died in that last horse versus pugilist boxing match, you decided you could never return to gambling again. And you’ll never forgive that horse who trampled him.
4. **Addiction**: Realizing you hit a low point when you started participating in Ooze Breed Shows, you called it cold turkey. You still feel bad for all the agility coursework you forced on Lil’ Ochre.
5. **Vengeance**: In a completely unfair turn, the sorcerer you won the pot from cursed you never to be able to play another hand.
6. **Boredom**: You no longer find any thrill in cards, dice, races, or anything of the like. You hope the dungeon can provide your new kicks.

### What was your grandest prize?

1. **Witch’s Tooth**: The last thing the bog hag could match you with was her divining tooth. She said if you rattle it around in your mouth when you spit it out, the patterns in your phlegm can be read by a seer.
2. **Foreign Currencies**: Over the years, some coins not realm-minted snuck their way into your winnings. You’re not even sure this stuff is real copper, silver, or gold, and it bears peculiar visages on each coin.
3. **Marble Wolf**: Under the direct light of a full moon, this miniature wolf statue will prowl around and follow your commands.
4. **Fancy Darts**: Oiled rosewood and polished brass adornments, you have a nice box with three extremely precise and impressive darts.
5. **Cantankerous Beast**: The local cat lady had a few to spare, and you were generous, so you agreed it matched your bet. Unfortunately, the feline never ended up liking you. It mostly just ignores you until it’s hungry. However, it becomes alert when anyone tries to sneak up on it.
6. **Circlet of the Dwarfs**: When placed upon a non-dwarf’s head, this slender silver circlet causes the wearer to grow a massive, stately beard. After removing the circlet, the beard proceeds to fall out in a fairly scratchy fashion over a week.

## Herbalist

### Why did you leave your job?

1. **Tormented**: Thinking you were ridding yourself of a garden-variety pest, you poisoned the pet mole of a particularly cantankerous gnome. They have since defiled your gardens and wilted your herbs whenever they could.
2. **Haunted**: When you were a child, one of your village playmates drowned while you two were playing in a lake. You never told their parents, and you have seen your friend’s face in the dirt mounds where you picked herbs for the past several years.
3. **Allergies**: You thought if you just searched long enough, you would find the cure to the sneezing you suffer when handling most roots and leaves. Unfortunately, this never happened.
4. **Assassin**: Every time you went looking for new plants, you were assaulted by murderous strands of sentient vines intent on using your body for fertilizer.
5. **Competition**: Shroom folk found their way to your village and would offer spores of themselves to the locals. It seemed to cure most ailments, and they disdained being paid for their services, rendering you out of work.
6. **Poisoned**: Stuck by the thorn of a corpse-thistle, you were bedridden for several months. While fighting for your life, you called to one of the many goddesses of gardens and promised you’d pick flourishing vegetation no more.

### What did you take with you?

1. **Amadou**: A peculiar fungus that quickly catches flame. You have about 6 uses worth.
2. **Cat’s Nip +1**: You keep this in a tightly-sealed bottle, but when you sprinkle even a pinch of it, a dozen or so local cats will find their way to it. Nearly any feline will be rendered catatonic for almost an hour after getting a taste. You have about ten pinches remaining.
3. **Tacky Stalk**: This woody reed contains a fibrous, chalky interior that may be chewed into a gummy wad. After drying out, this wad hardens entirely, becoming a permanent adhesive.
4. **Artificial Flowers**: You’re not entirely sure what this is, but it seems to be a bouquet made of felt, wax, wire, and other materials. It is resplendent and looks natural if it’s not closely observed.
5. **Seed Bomb x3**: You have three dirt clods held together in small canvas sacks. Their contents will explode on impact, showering whatever they strike with dirt and numerous irritating seeds, befuddling anyone hit. After a bit of rain, a patch of wildflowers appears in a week or so.
6. **Jovial Mandrake**: A small root that looks like a tiny human. This being follows you, telling jokes and laughing non-stop in its shrill, high-pitched voice.

## Magician

### Why did you quit magic?

1. **Prosecuted**: A series of witch hunters swore to have you drowned.
2. **Debt**: While performing a routine stage show, a being born from the shadows cast by your candles offered you power for your soul. Mistaking it as a potential part of your act, you obliged.
3. **Loss**: You mastered making things disappear from within your pointed hat, but you never found out where they went. Eventually, every pet you ever had was lost to the ether.
4. **Heckler**: One incredibly insensitive lay-about child always snuck into your shows and would shout the worst jibes to the enjoyment of your measly crowd.
5. **Sabotage**: Someone snuck into your storage and ruined your saw box, leading to your on-stage dismemberment of an audience participant.
6. **Thievery**: The look-alike you used for tricks stole your entire routine and has completely drawn your crowd away from you, painting you as the fraud.

### What trick was your specialty?

1. **Mentalism**: You have a significant chance of being able to guess a card drawn by another being from a deck in your control.
2. **Escapery**: Knots pose only a moderate threat to your freedom. You can also regurgitate a swallowed key or small item with a repulsive ability.
3. **Death Defiance**: Surviving being submerged for several minutes was something you routinely practiced, or at least you can suppress panicking before losing consciousness.
4. **Levitation**: Floating a stationary target above a table weighing no more than a cat was a trick you received the least lukewarm applause for.
5. **Iron Jaws**: You’ve discovered the correct technique of eating glass and a variety of other traditionally inedible substances.
6. **Spiritualism**: With a bit of prior setup, smoke, and mirrors, you have a good chance of convincing someone that a partner of yours is their deceased loved one.

## Mariner

### Why is it hard to step back onto a ship?

1. **Abandonment**: The deal you made with the merfolk to marry one of their own in exchange for sea riches turned out to be a double disaster—the riches were just a decade’s supply of pungent kelp, and your betrothed was a ravenous deep one. Leaving a fish beast at the altar is slight that the sea shan’t soon forgive.
2. **Disfigurement**: Deciding that two peg legs are enough, you’ll try your lot on land.
3. **Fired**: Your cantankerous sea shanties were decreed a nuisance. That, and your constant chugging of rum despite your penchant for sea sickness, had you fired from your position on the ship—literally, as your captain strapped you to a catapult and launched you ashore.
4. **Cursed**: Ever since you decided to use gulls as target practice, you’ve felt that the sea had it out for you. Constantly tossed overboard by storm, harassed by sharks, and even once, an octopus climbed aboard to strangle you. You feel that keeping your distance from water is for the best.
5. **Jonahesque**: Your ship was smashed, and you were swallowed whole by a leviathan. You became accustomed to the horrors of the dark as you traversed the belly of the beast. Only by joining a party of a wooden golem, a talking insect, and a wizened craftsman were you able to escape.
6. **Change**: You found yourself weary of the constant rocking of waves, damp quarters, and the smell of fish. You decided stable ground would be far more adventurous than long months on an urchin-crusted vessel.

### What memento do you carry with you?

1. **Clam of Devouring**: A small mollusk with a serious appetite, this shelled creature will clamp down on anything it can get its shell around. It will then slowly begin to digest whatever it has caught.
2. **Sea Monkey**: Despite appearing as a flask of salt water, the watery contents transform into a moderately disobedient monkey who desperately doesn’t want to be put back into to its container.
3. **Boat Terrorizing Flag**: A black flag you stole off a notorious pirate ship. This flag allows you to turn boats as a cleric might turn the undead.
4. **Eye-Patch of Night Seeing**: This mystical eyepiece gifts you minor low-light vision at the cost all depth perception.
5. **Land Remora**: Not entirely clear how this fish survives outside water; this eel-like creature wriggles all around your body, nibbling at crumbs, flakes of dried skin, and loose threads on your clothing.
6. **First Mate**: A not exceptionally bright sailor from your mariner days follows you as long as they are fed, providing low-quality manual labor and high-quality commentary on all your mistakes.

## Mercenary

### What is your shame?

1. **Fired**: Your routine shirking of duties, going missing, and just plain ornery nature led to you getting kicked out of the company. Everyone but one of your old sympathetic pals looks down upon you.
2. **Shell-shocked**: Your company was one of the first to acquire a dragonpowder bombard. Still trying to figure out how it worked, they launched you from it. You now smell faintly of smoke and sulfur no matter how much you wash.
3. **Snitched On**: The wolves your company used as scouts saw you taking a bit of extra coin from the company’s coffers and dutifully made their best Lassie impression to point you out. You’re sure one wolf still pursues you despite your being cashiered.
4. **Last Mercenary Standing**: You were ordered to slay the last defender of a conquered fortress. The figure transformed before your eyes, taking the shape of a massive minotaur, brutally slaying all your company. Somehow you hid in the rubble and gore and survived but swore vengeance upon this shapeshifter.
5. **Laughing Stock**: Your preference for ridiculous and sometime self-injuring weapons such as massive flails, impractical whips, chain staves, and the like got ridiculed by all your company. You decided to wander off, the lone wolf you are, to prove to them that your prototype boomerang crossbow will be the next advancement in warfare.
6. **Undead**: Your previous mission to the Crypt of the Dread Necromancer ended unexpectedly with your gruesome death. What was surprising, however, was that you arose that very night, a member of the unliving. Aside from your slight odor and an inability to bear the sight of holy symbols, you are mostly the same. You feared persecution and decided to flee your company.

### What treasure did you find on your final mission?

1. **Sarcastic Suit of Armor**: You found a set of filigreed plate armor, but it seems to be related to those fabled singing swords, as it always has some disparaging remark or joke about whatever you’re doing. This is incredibly maddening as it flaps its visor up and down whenever it talks, even while you’re wearing it.
2. **Rodent of Radiant Charisma**: While marching through the marshlands, you fell under the enchanting spell of a capybara. This creature tags along with you and calms most mundane animals when not too much commotion occurs.
3. **Handgonne**: An extremely rare metal tube set into a heavy block. You can produce an extremely dangerous blast if you stuff this full of black powder and various sharp or metallic objects. Sometimes that danger affects you and your compatriots. You weren’t fortunate enough to receive any instruction in its proper use.
4. **Reverse Umbrella**: You drew the shortest straw, so all your companions got the first pick of the defeated wizard’s armory. This umbrella produces a localized rainstorm when it’s open, pouring continuously out of the umbrella.
5. **An Opulent Hat**: Full of feathers, pins, and adornments, this massive hat is always the center of conversation.
6. **Harpy Egg**: Everyone told you not to take it. Anger the harpies, they said. You’d be the death of us, they said. Well, they were right. But that will not stop you from being a momma soon.

## Merchant

### How was your career ruined?

1. **Ridiculous inventory**: Very few people are interested in purchasing “The deed to one goblin.”
2. **Cursed product**: That weird sky rock you sold to the baron cracked, besetting the fief with vapors of an intelligible color. You decided to skip town before anyone traced the source.
3. **Lost investment**: The spice ship you investing everything for almost reached the Duchy before the marauding merfolk wrecked it. If only you had enough coin to hire muscle to retrieve the wreckage.
4. **Limitless discount**: Your initial idea of giving someone a coupon redeemable 10% off a purchase for every 100 gold pieces spent drew many return customers. Then someone thought to cash in 10 at once and take everything.
5. **Hunted**: Your specialty of monster teeth and claws came back to bite you in more ways than one when a troll regenerated from a poorly bleached fang.
6. **Planar legal fees** Thinking this nasty-looking silver sword you were traded by an adventurer would net you a pretty penny, you were met with a headache when a strange-looking figure stepped out of a portal threatening legal action as your “crystal sphere” didn’t have the license associated with selling such a weapon.

### What was one exciting trade that you kept?

1. **Riddle Skull**: A human skull that responds to the words “riddle me” with a unique riddle once per day. Getting the riddle wrong enrages the skull, which spends half an hour trying to bite you. You were told something extraordinary would happen if you answered correctly three days in a row but have yet to do so.
2. **Rodent Automata**: Brass and tin mechanical mouse. Will follow instructions of up to three sentences spoken to it before being wound up. Afterward, it will buzz, hum, and complete the task as best as a tiny clockwork mouse can. Winding only sustains it for about ten minutes.
3. **Wand of Clutter**: Gesturing this wand at a sorted collection of dishware, clothing, or any nick-knacks will compel the items to get up, run around in disarray and come to rest in a disorganized mess over a 10-foot by 10-foot area.
4. **Tricorn of True North**: A decent hat that will rotate atop your head, always pointing north when worn.
5. **Iridescent Salamander**: A small lizard-like creature that secretes a luminescent fluid once a day when squeezed. This substance glows with about the radiance of a candle in pastel colors. Unfortunately, the lizard also has a vicious bite whenever squeezed.
6. **Man’s Best Boot**: This single old leather boot follows you with the utmost loyalty and even lolls its tongue like a dog. It cannot bark but will excitedly tap or stomp the ground.

## Outlaw

### What ended your run from the law?

1. **Snitching**: You reported on fellow scoundrels. Your name was cleared, but you made a few enemies.
2. **Humiliated**: You were captured, put into the stockades, and the whole town got a good laugh by throwing rotten food at you for a week. Now the urchins point and laugh, calling you Stinky Cabbage.
3. **Conscience**: The first time you tried to rob anything, a small child looked at you in shame; you have since recanted your ways, fearing the gaze of the innocent.
4. **Remuneration**: You turned yourself in and were fined everything you own. Routinely you witness the Baron’s snotty son misusing your former prized possessions.
5. **Loophole**: It turns out your perceived crimes were not crimes. You wasted all that time living off the land, so you need to make the oncoming years count, and everyone feels pity for you.
6. **Betrayal**: You strolled into town and made a bald-faced lie that you were framed. Nobody liked the miller who suffered the wrath of your pointed finger. Still, many say his daughter has declared revenge upon you.

### What weird trinket did you stumble upon on your journeys?

1. **Eternal Hat**: The hat you wear on your head always produces a new hat underneath when you remove the previous one. Every hat is distasteful and out of style. Since putting the initial hat on, you haven’t figured out how to bare your head. None of the removed hats retain this odd effect.
2. **Comedic Apple**: This piece of fruit won’t stop telling groan-worthy jokes and puns that barely work. You think your companions are going to eat it out of annoyance.
3. **A Very Stupid Parrot**: This gorgeous bird is willing to follow commands but always misinterprets them in astonishingly bad ways.
4. **Fireworks**: You stole a crate of moldy fireworks from a wagon. Less than half a dozen still function, although not predictably.
5. **Lasso of Fibs**: The victim may not say anything true when bound by this rope.
6. **Tin Can of Partial Message**: A short phrase may be spoken into this tin can, and then the next time someone holds it up to their ear, they hear it. It’s also very strict about pronunciation. You almost feel like it’s judging you when “espresso” is said as “expresso.”

## Performer

### What was your final performance?

1. **Avant-garde**: Your experimental one-person-show perplexed even the most diabolical coalition of warlocks who cursed you never to play again.
2. **Offensive**: The prince did not enjoy the crowd’s laughter responding to your musical accusations of his romantic failings.
3. **Injury**: The exotic beast seller promised you that your purchased owlbear was toothless. Turns out beaks are just as deadly.
4. **Maddening**: A traveling astronomer sold you sheet music inspired by vibrations they claimed to hear while looking into the night sky. Your fluting turned the audience into a gibbering, howling mob, rasping in fear and admiration of something called “the dwellers beyond the veil of space.” Not exactly your troupe’s name, so you didn’t take it as praise.
5. **Timidity**: Your only performance involved you staring wide-eyed into the audience for several minutes. When you finally opened your mouth to speak your lines, you were caught in anxiety-induced dry heaving. Everyone uncomfortably got up and walked out.
6. **Cursed**: The various poems you recited mocked a variety of unnatural beings and their believers as delusional. A wayward demi-god found your atheism disquieting and cursed you to be a magnet for the supernatural.

### What trinket did you take from your troupe?

1. **Simian Skull**: Sometimes confused for monster bones, this fanged skull smells faintly of exotic fruits.
2. **Massive Prop Weapon**: A gnarly blade resembling a weapon of the gods. It’s much more intimidating until it’s revealed to be a wood-and-wool replica.
3. **Speculative Future Clothing**: A peculiar cotton t-shaped tunic and rough blue trousers with a peculiar buckling apparatus near the front of the waist. This outfit is based on a fictional codex of a time-traveling serf in the far future.
4. **Tome of Troubling Limericks**: These verses become less bawdy the further you get into the book and become more and more harrowing.
5. **Many Faced Mask**: This leather and plaster mask appears mundane, but every time it is slipped off, it takes the countenance of a random creature of legend.
6. **Stage Double**: Seeing how you are out of work, your double is also. They continue to follow you day to day, but not without regularly complaining that you ruined their career.

## Pickpocket

### How were you caught?

1. **Lack of Finesse**: Walking right up to someone, holding direct eye contact, and shoving your hands straight into their coin purse is a great way to be immediately arrested.
2. **Mini Man Alarm**: Bit by a pocket homunculus, your yelps gave you away.
3. **Outplayed**: Your last mark was a master counter-thief. The mark somehow stole every last thing you owned.
4. **Betrayed**: One of your companions ratted you out to the sheriff, who used your capture as a feather in their cap.
5. **Loud Mouthed**: After buying several rounds of ales, a stranger asked you how you came upon such good fortunes. You quickly learned that honesty is not always the best policy.
6. **Divination**: After retiring from a career of adventuring, the local hedge mage decided to become a village sleuth.

### What was the oddest thing you lifted?

1. **Tiny Totem of Confidence**: A figure of a local deity carved from smooth stone. When its belly is rubbed, you can hear affirmations in your head.
2. **Digestible Treasure**: One purse full of imitation coins, the gold nothing but foil. The chocolate internals, at least, are a rare treat.
3. **Psychedelic Mold**: The leather purse your stole from a visiting druid contained only patches of moist growth inside. Ingesting this made you aware of the secret lives of colors and the various polyhedral shapes living in-between wood grains.
4. **Small Pouch of Devouring**: Fanged on the inside, this bag whines unless provided with a weekly meal. It digests any organic and inorganic material you put into it without producing waste. Or at least without producing waste as of yet.
5. **Monster Teeth**: Unsettlingly, the local miller had a purse bursting with various fangs, teeth, and claws. You are unsure whether you should report them to someone or if you can use these in some ritual.
6. **Lovely Lich’s Locket**: Unsure who it belonged to, but someone in your village kept a heart-shaped locket with a rendition of the Dread Lich Pestulentia. Can you use this to your advantage?

## Smuggler

### Why did you stop smuggling?

1. **Pursued**: A paladin of the lawful deities of economies & trade swore an oath to see you apprehended. Fortunately, they are still tied up getting the correct permits to come after you, but you fear the day they get their paperwork in order.
2. **Unnatural Encounter**: Delivering trinkets to a shadowy cove, your patron revealed itself to be a tentacled thinking fish who psychically monologued about drenching the surface to return the planet to the control of an oceanic empire.
3. **Blackmailed**: The local rogue’s guild threatened to go directly to the Bishop, your uncle, with proof that you have been delivering relics to the cults of Chaos. They now demand you seek out treasures for them.
4. **Revenge**: Appalled to discover that the “rare heirloom bottles” the dark-robed figures had you trek back and forth between coteries contained the spirits of the deceased, you swore a promise to free any remaining soul part-damned by your deeds.
5. **Catastrophe**: Your entire supply sank deep underwater, the duke you were delivering it to demands you pay, and the clam-folk are infuriated at the pollution you caused.
6. **Addicted**: Having made it big muggling a scarce fungal growth to wealthy merchants of the port cities, you tried a sample, brewing mushroom tea. The other realms of existence and reality it revealed to you were intoxicating. Still, the price of a single serving is so high that you knew you had to get as far away as possible.

### What’s one item that “fell off a wagon?”

1. **Frost Giant’s Spit**: A bottle of azure liquid that stays exceedingly cold and can temporarily freeze anything it is poured upon. Enough fluid remains to coat a hypothetical forearm.
2. **Dark Creeper’s Night Soil**: This bag of foul-smelling fertilizer causes seeds or bulbs to be fully grown within 24 hours. You hope the name is just a joke.
3. **Insurrectionist Doom Tablets**: Clay tablets pressed with a worrying amount of praise to the dark gods, coupled with some excellent criticisms of the exploitation done by nobles and clergy.
4. **Displacer Beetles**: A tiny box filled with beetles that are always closer or further away than they appear.
5. **Faulty Holy Symbols**: A bunch of vampires tied to circulate holy symbols that would fail mid-turning among the clerical order. You have about a half dozen of these.
6. **Shrieker’s Treat**: One vial of a poison that forces its victim to continually scream until passing out.

## Student

### What ended your studies?

1. **Tuition**: After an adventuring crew wrecked the local economy, the academy prices have steadily risen. You could not pay last semester’s rates, but maybe you could find the crypt those adventurers were whispering about.
2. **Forbidden Studies**: Your latest term paper startled the faculty. The model of the metaverse you presented, lorded over by a half-dozen capricious deities playing dice with the universe, got you immediately expelled.
3. **Bullying**: Stuffed into a locker for the last time, you have decided to find a magic item to prove to your ruffian classmates that you have more nerve than they believed.
4. **Academic Cult**: Staying late in the library one night, you heard a commotion in the commons and tip-toed to check it out. The entire faculty was surrounding a horrific obsidian idol. They removed their faces, revealing themselves as some worm or centipede creatures posing as teachers.
5. **Doppelganger**: An alchemical experiment gave life to an evil twin of yourself. The academy only had room for one student, though, and your doppelganger made quick friends with the faculty, and they decided it would remain in your place. The final straw was when you returned home, and your parents were dining and laughing with the clone.
6. **Lack of Languages**: Despite your best efforts, you haven’t been able to speak the dozens of required fantastical languages. Maybe if you encountered them in an environment outside the classroom, you could catch up.

### What was something you never returned to your academy’s tower before leaving?

1. **Technicality Imp**: Former familiar of one of your instructors, this annoying little creature always corrects people with the most specific interpretation of topics discussed.
2. **Cypher Satchel**: Shoulder bag that jostles letters around in any book contained within. Ordinary books are reduced to incoherent messes. When a jumbled book is placed back into the satchel and shaken around, the letters return to their normal position.
3. **Inverted Chalk**: A stick of chalk that consumes writing instead of producing it.
4. **History of Known Traps**: While not going into the mechanical workings of any particular trap, this tome describes the history, trends, and creators of various dungeon hazards.
5. **Monocle of Dust and Mites**: A single lens that reveals the precise quantities of dust in the nearby environs and allows the wearer to peer into the secret lives of the microscopic fairies that dirty all our things. Everything else viewed with this monocle on is indecipherably blurry.
6. **Blood Dowsing Rod**: Creepy forked stick that points to the largest source of blood within thirty feet.

## Tracker

### Why have you quit?

1. **Cursed**: A gigantic blue elk with golden glowing eyes approached as you took your final catch, promising to hunt you to the end of your days if you stepped foot into its forest again.
2. **Hunting Accident**: Your younger sibling would invite you to hunt every time your parent spoke of passing down the family’s wealth to you. You finally got the message when the crossbow bolts kept narrowly missing your head and disclaimed your inheritance.
3. **Broken Heart**: After your beloved old bloodhound couldn’t follow you on hunts any longer, you felt it wasn’t worth it.
4. **Nobles**: All the lords decried the forests of the land as their domain, forbidding any others to hunt within, putting you out of a job.
5. **Wizardslaughter**: You didn’t mean to kill the village’s most-loved and kindest-hearted wizened old druid. You had no idea he polymorphed himself into fauna to frolic in the woods.
6. **Haunting**: The dooming blight that choked many lands has reached your ranges, littering the once peaceful groves with hungry ghosts.

### What keepsake did you retain?

1. **Worm-Calling Whistle**: When blown, the high-pitched whistle attracts around a dozen earthworms, grubs, and other dirt-crawling insects.
2. **False Boomerang**: This heavily-weathered boomerang not only does not return to you but seems to travel unerringly in a straight line forever until it crashes into something. You’ve been worried you’ll lose it if you throw it toward the sky.
3. **Bear Suit**: The entire furs of a person-sized bear, treated and stitched to be wearable. It’s hard to see out of the beast’s snout, but not the worst disguise from afar.
4. **Game Spookers**: A very weighty round brass bell. Toss into a bush to scare out any game or dangle upon your walking stick to give yourself jaunty entrance music.
5. **Snare Kit**: A heavy canvas sack filled with all manner of traps: iron teeth, lassos, ropes, nets, counterweights, and snares.
6. **Salts & Spices**: A very fine wooden box filled with tubes and pouches of uncommon spices, treating salts, and fancy woods. Suitable for preserving meat or adding a bit of pizazz to any campfire meal.
