At the beginning of a round, the GM describes the most likely actions to be taken by your opponents. You then declare your character’s intentions for the round before rolling any dice. 

If you attempt something risky, the GM may call for a save. If two combatants attempt to overcome the other, whoever is most at risk rolls the save.

---

Stunts

Stunts are combat maneuvers such as stunning, shoving, disarming, tripping, sundering armor, and so on. They are resolved with a versus save. They may not cause damage directly, but may do so indirectly (for example, pushing an enemy off of a ledge). The GM is the final arbiter as to what stunts can be attempted in a given situation.

Advantage in Combat

Characters can gain advantage in combat by attacking a target that is unaware, on lower ground, off balance, disarmed, distracted, or tactically disadvantaged in any significant way. The GM, as usual, has the final say.

When a character has advantage against an opponent on their combat turn, they may either A.) Apply advantage to their attack roll or stunt against that opponent or B.) Make an attack and a stunt attempt in the same round against that opponent, without advantage

Close: Melee weapons.

Near: Medium ranged weapons. Far: Large ranged weapons.

Each ability is used in different circumstances:

- STR: Used for saves requiring physical power, like lifting, breaking, and resisting poison.
- DEX: Used for saves requiring poise, speed, and reflexes, like dodging, climbing, sneaking, and balancing.
- WILL: Used for saves to persuade, deceive, interrogate, intimidate, charm, provoke, and invoke spiritual or magical power.

### Weapons

Some weapons have keywords that affect their use.

- **Ammo**: This weapon requires ammunition to be carried in a body inventory slot. Otherwise, only a single attack can be made.
- **Long-ranged**: This weapon can attack a target up to 3 zones away.
- **Short-ranged**: This weapon can attack targets up to 2 zones away.
- **Thrown**: This weapon can be used to attack targets in the same zone or thrown at a target in an adjacent zone.
- **Two-handed**: This weapon takes up both hand slots.
- **Versatile**: This weapon has two entries noted under damage; it deals the first when wielded with one hand and the second when wielded with two.

### Armor

Armor grants an Armor Rating (AR), which reduces incoming damage. A character can never have more than 6 AV, even if items would increase it above this number.

Wielding a shield increases AR but reduces Weapon Speed.
